by

Spermatogenesis refers to the creation of sperm cells. It is the process by which a normal diploid cell splits into four and eventually matures to become a full fledged spermatozoon.

This website will trace the path of a sperm cell from its production in the testis to its eventual ejaculation.

Part 1

Production of Spermatids

In the seminiferous tubules within the testes, primordial germ cells divide mitotically, with one of its daughter cells being a primary spermatocyte.

The primary spermatocyte then divides meiotically: from a diploid cell with two sets of chromosomes, it creates four haploid spermatids with only one set of chromosomes.

The seminiferous tubules' location within the testes is extremely important for spermatogenesis, as sperm can only be produced under lower temperatures such as those provided by the insulation of the scrotum.

Part 2

Spermatid Maturation

scroll down

After meiosis, the immotile spermatids are surrounded by testicular fluid, causing them to migrate from the seminiferous tubules to the epididymis for maturation and storage.

scroll down
The human epididymis.
image source (link)

Maturation sculpts the distinctive characteristics of sperm, turning a normal-looking, circular spermatid into a spermatozoon.

scroll down
A spermatozoon, the mature version of a spermatid.
image source (link)

During maturation, the spermatid's Golgi apparatus disintegrates, forming the acrosome, while it begins to grow a sperm cell's distinctive tail.

Part 3

Erection

scroll down

During ejaculation, mature sperm travels from the epididymis to the vas deferens through smooth muscle contractions.

scroll down

The vas deferens is a tube-like structure leading toward the seminal vesicle. As sperm travel through it, the penis fills with blood and becomes erect.

Part 4

Seminal Fluid Secretion

scroll down

When sperm cells arrive at the appula of the vas deferens, it is mixed with seminal fluid secreted from the seminal vesicle.

scroll down

This seminal fluid, which accounts for around 60% of semen volume, it mostly composed of fructose. The presence of fructose allows a sperm cell's mitochondrion to generate energy in the form of ATP, which enables sperm cells to move through the female reproductive tract.

Part 5

Synthesis of Semen

scroll down

The last stop before ejaculation is the prostate gland, which secretes a milky, alkaline fluid that combines with seminal fluid to complete ingredients of semen.

scroll down

This milky fluid makes up 30% of total semen volume. Its alkalinity neutralizes the acidity of the vaginal tract, allowing sperm to survive longer.
